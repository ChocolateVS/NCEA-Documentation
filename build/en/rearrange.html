Re-arranging Formula

Rearranging formula or rule means we write a given letter in terms of another letter with numbers (.i.e. solve the equation for the unknown).

For example, Write \( {(x - 1) \over (x + 1)} = t \) in terms of t

.
Show Answer

\( {(x - 1) \over (x + 1)} = t \)


Multiply both sides by th denominator (x + 1):

\( x - 1 = t(x + 1) \)


Expand:

\( x - 1 = tx + t \)


Add 1 to each side

\( x = tx + t + 1 \)


Subtract tx from each side

\( x - tx = t + 1 \)


Factorise LHS

\( x(1 - t) = t + 1 \)


Divide both sides by (1 - t)

\( x = {(t + 1) \over (1 - t)}; t \ne 1\)


For the given rule \( {y \over (x - 1)} = {2y \over (3x - 4t)} \) write t as the subject

.

\( {y \over (x - 1)} = {2y \over (3x - 4t)}\)


Use fraction multiplying rule: if

\( {a \over b} = {c \over d}, a * d = c * b\)


\( y(3x - 4t) = 2y(x - 1) \)


Divide both sides by y:

\( 3x - 4t = 2(x - 1) \)


Expand:

\( 3x - 4t = 2x - 2 \)


Subtract 3x from both sides:

\( -4t = -x - 2 \)


Divide by -4

\( t = {-x - 2 \over -4} \)


\( t = {x + 2 \over 4} \)
