Surds (Roots)

Surds are such expressions as

\(\sqrt{7}\sqrt[3]{40}\)

which cannot be evaluated exactly. They are irrational. Expressions involving surds are simplified.

Rules

The following rules apply:

1. \(\sqrt{a}\ * \sqrt{b}\ = \sqrt{ab}\)

2. \(\sqrt{a}\ \div \sqrt{b}\ = \sqrt{a \div b}\)

3. \(a\sqrt{c} \pm b\sqrt{c} = (a \pm b)\sqrt{c} \) (like surds can be added or subtracted)

Note:

\(\sqrt{a} * \sqrt{a} = a\)

\((\sqrt{a})^2 = a\)

\(\sqrt{a} \pm \sqrt{b} \ne \sqrt{(a \pm b)}\) for non-zero a and b

To simplify a surd like square roots we have to write the number inside the surd as product of two factors, one factor has to be a largest square number of that surd.

For Example:

\( \sqrt{75} = \sqrt{25 * 3} = \sqrt{25} * \sqrt{3} = 5\sqrt{3} \)


For Example:

\( 3\sqrt{2} - \sqrt{8} = 3\sqrt{2} - \sqrt{4 * 2} = 3\sqrt{2} - \sqrt{4} * \sqrt{2} = 3\sqrt{2} - 2\sqrt{2} = \sqrt{2} \)

When fractions are involved with surds, it is the normal practice to eliminate the surds from the denominator; this called rationalising the denominator (ie. clearing it of irrational numbers.)

For expression of the type

\( c \over \sqrt{a} \)

, we multiply the top and the bottom by

\( \sqrt{a} \)

giving:

\( {c \over \sqrt{a}} = {c \over \sqrt{a}} * {\sqrt{a} \over \sqrt{a}} = {c\sqrt{a} \over a} \)

Conjugate Surds

Conjugate surds are surds which will differ in sign, The rule only applies to binomial (two terms) expressions
A conjugate surd is aquired by replacing the sign with the inverse For Example:

Binomial Expression

\( \leftrightarrow \)

Corresponding Conjugate

\( a + \sqrt{b} \leftrightarrow a - \sqrt{b} \)


\( a - \sqrt{b} \leftrightarrow a + \sqrt{b} \)


\( \sqrt{a} + b \leftrightarrow \sqrt{a} - b\)


\( \sqrt{a} - b \leftrightarrow \sqrt{a} + b\)


Simplifying binomial surds written as fraction

Consider

\({1 \over b + \sqrt{a}}\)

, we multiply the top and the bottom by

\(b - \sqrt{a}\)

, which is called the conjugate.

\({1 \over b + \sqrt{a}} = {1 \over (b + \sqrt{a})} * {b - \sqrt{a} \over b - \sqrt{a}} = {b - \sqrt{a} \over b^2 - (\sqrt{a})^2} = {b - \sqrt{a} \over b^2 - a^2}\)
