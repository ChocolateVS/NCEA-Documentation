Completing the square

It means that you write the quadratic expression,

\( ax^2 + bx +c \)

in the form a

\(a(x+p)^2 + q\)

where a, b, c, p, and q are real numbers.
For example, in the expansion of

\( (x + b)^2 = x^2 + 2bx + b^2 \)


Notice that the last term,

\( b^2 \)

is the square of half the coefficient of x, (2b).

Method


See that the coefficient of

\( x^2 \)

is 1 always, if it is not 1 then adjust the coefficient to 1.
Add a box squared to bx and subtract a box squared from c.
Divide the coefficient of x by 2 always, the answer will go in the square boxes.
Rewrite the expression as

\( (x + p)^2 + q\)

or

\( a(x + p)^2 + q\)


Example:

Complete the square on

\( x^2 + 6x + 15 \)


Answer
Show Answer

\( x^2 + 6x + 15 = x^2 + 6x + \boxed{}^2 + 15 - \boxed{}^2\)


\( = (x + 3)^2 + 15 - 9 \)


\( = (x + 3)^2 + 6\)


Example:

Complete the square on

\( 3x^2 + 12x + 27 \)


Answer
Show Answer

\( 3x^2 + 12x + 27 = 3(x^2 + 4x + 9)\)


\( = 3((x + 2)^2 + 9 - 2^2) \)


\( = 3((x + 2)^2 + 5) \)


\( = 3(x + 2)^2 + 15\)
