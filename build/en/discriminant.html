Discriminant Function

The discriminant of a quadratic equation is:

\( \varDelta = b^2 - 4ac \)

.
The root of any quadratic equation depends on the discriminant value:
- For one real root the discriminant is 0 (

\( \varDelta = 0 \)

)
- For two real roots the discriminant is greater than 0 (i.e.

\( \varDelta \gt 0 \)

).
- For no roots the discriminant is less than 0 (i.e.

\( \varDelta \lt 0 \)

).

Example of Discriminant Function use

For what values(s) of p does:

\( x^2 + {x \over p} + 1 = 0\) have equal roots

.
Show Answer Equal roots means one real root so:

\(b^2 - 4ac = 0\)


\( ({1 \over p})^2 -4(1)(1) = 0 \)

, substitute a = 1, b = 1/p and c = 1 into discriminant equation

\( ({1 \over p})^2 -4 = 0 \)


\( ({1 \over p})^2 = 4 \)


\( {1 \over p} = 2 \)


\( p = {1 \over 2} = 0.5\)



Find one value of s for which:

\( x^2 + 2(s + 3) x + (s^2 + 2s - 5) \gt 0\), has real roots

.
Show Answer Real roots means discrimanant is greater than 0:

\(b^2 - 4ac = 0\)


\( a = 1\)


\( b = 2(s + 3) \)


\( c = (s^2 + 2s - 5) \)


\( 2(s + 3)^2 - 4(1)(s^2 + 2s - 5) > 0 \)

, substitute a = 1, b = 1/p and c = 1 into discriminant equation

\( (2s + 6)(2s + 6) - 4s^2 - 8s + 20 > 0\)


\( 4s^2 + 24s + 36 - 4s^2 - 8s + 20 > 0\)

, Expand

\( 16s + 56 > 0 \)


\( 16s > -56 \)


\( s > {-56 \over 16} \)


\( s > {-7 \over 2} \)


\( s > -3.5 \)
