Applying the idea of solving equations to complex number

Rearranging formula or rule means we write a given letter in terms of another letter with numbers (.i.e. solve the equation for the unknown).
The idea is to rearrange the equation with unknowns on one side and number parts on the other side.
Equate the real parts of both sides and imaginary parts of both sides, of the equation.
Solve for the unknowns.

\( \color{blue}{Blue = Real.}\)


\( \color{green}{Green = Imaginary.}\)


For example

\( \color{blue}{2x} + \color{blue}{3} + \color{green}{2iy} \color{black}{=} \color{green}{8i} + \color{blue}{5y} \)

.
Rearrange so unknowns are on the left, and knowns are on the right hand side of the equation

\( \color{blue}{2x} - \color{blue}{5y} + \color{green}{2iy} \color{black}{=} \color{green}{8i} - \color{blue}{3} \)


Real Parts:

\( \color{blue}{2x} - \color{blue}{5y} \color{black}{=} - \color{blue}{3} \)


Imaginary Parts (excluding i):

\( \color{green}{2y} \color{black}{=} \color{green}{8} \)


Solve for y:

\( \color{green}{y} \color{black}{=} \color{green}{4} \)


Replace y into real equation to solve for x

\( \color{blue}{2x} - \color{blue}{5\color{green}{(4)}} \color{black}{=} - \color{blue}{3} \)


\( \color{blue}{2x} \color{black}{=} \color{blue}{17} \)


\( \color{blue}{x} \color{black}{=} \color{blue}{17 / 2} \)


For example

\( \sqrt{x + yi} = 1 - i \)

.
Square both sides:

\( x + yi = (1 - i)^2 \)


\( x + yi = 1 - 2i + i^2 \)


Remebering

\( i^2 = -1\)


\( x + yi = 0 - 2i \)


Real Part:

\( \color{blue}{x = 0} \)


Imaginary Part:

\( \color{green}{y = 2i} \)
