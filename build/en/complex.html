Complex numbers

Under real number system square roots of negative numbers does not exist. But in complex number system it is possible to find square roots of negative numbers by doing the following substitution.

\( i = \sqrt{-1}\) or \( i^2 = -1 \)

A complex number is made of real and imaginary numbers. We represent the set of complex numbers by letter C. The letter z represent arbitrary complex number.

Any complex number z is written as \( z = x + yi\) or \( z = a + bi \).

We call x, the real part of z written as

\( x = Re(z) \).


We call y, the imaginary part of z written as

\( y = Im(z) \).


Note: That Im(z) does not include i, it is just the coefficient of i.
For Example, find the Real, Re(z), and Imaginary, Im(z), parts given complex numbers

\( z = 2 - 6i \), \(Re(z) = 2, Im(z) = 6 \).


\( z = {3 + 5i \over 6}\), \(Re(z) = {3 \over 6}, Im(z) = {5 \over 6}\).


\( z = (2 - i)(3 + 4i) = 6 - 3i + 8i - 4i^2 = 6 + 5i + 4 = 10 + 5i \), Re(z) = 10, Im(z) = 5 \).

Adding and Subtracting Complex Numbers

In adding or subtracting two or more complex numbers, ADD the real parts and ADD the imaginary parts of the complex numbers.

Example: If

\(z = 2 - 5i \)

and \( w = -3 + 7i\).


Find:

\((z + w) = (2 - 5i) + (-3 + 7i) = -1 + 2i\).


Find:

\((z - w) = (2 - 5i) - (-3 + 7i) = 5 - 12i\).


Find:

\((3z + 2w) = 3(2 - 5i) + 2(-3 + 7i) = (6 - 15i) + (-6 + 14i) = -i\).

Complex Conjugate

Every complex number z has a conjugate number denoted as

\( \bar z \)


If

\(z = a + bi\)

then its conjugate is \( \bar z = a - bi\).


For Example:

\(z = 4 - 2i\)

so

\( \bar z = 4 + 2i \)


Multiplying Complex Numbers

Expand (multiply) the brackets and write the answer in the form

\( x + yi \)


Calculate:

\( (2 + 3i)(1 - 5i) = 2 - 10i + 3i - 15i^2 = 2 + 7i -10(-1) = 12 + 7i \)


Dividing Complex Numbers

Multiply the numerator and denominator of the fraction by the conjugate of the denominator. Write your final answer in the form

\( x + yi \)


Calculate:

\( {3 - 5i \over 1 - i} = {3 - 5i \over 1 - i} * {1 + i \over 1 + i} = {(3 - 5i)(1 + i) \over (1 - i)(1 + i)} = {3 - 5i + 3i - i^2 \over 1 -i + i -i^2} = {3 - 2i + 1 \over 1 + 1} = {4 - 2i \over 2} = 2 - i \)
